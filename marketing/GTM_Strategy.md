# ResuCraft Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy

## 1. Executive Summary
ResuCraft is a privacy-first, real-time resume builder designed to help job seekers create professional, ATS-friendly resumes purely in the browser. This GTM strategy focuses on organic growth through content marketing, community engagement, and a Product Hunt launch, positioning the tool as a superior, no-nonsense alternative to bloated, paywalled resume builders.

## 2. Target Audience
*   **Primary Segment**: Recent college graduates and entry-level professionals (Gen Z) who need a quick, standard, and professional resume.
*   **Secondary Segment**: Tech professionals and developers who appreciate a clean, markdown-like editing experience and "Harvard style" simplicity.
*   **Pain Points Solved**:
    *   Frustration with "free" builders that ask for payment at the download screen.
    *   Concern over data privacy (uploading personal info to random servers).
    *   Difficulty seeing how changes look in real-time.

## 3. Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)
1.  **Truly Free & Private**: No login required, no data stored on servers. Everything stays in the browser.
2.  **Real-Time Preview**: Split-screen design lets users see their resume update instantly as they type.
3.  **ATS-Optimized**: Uses a clean, standard layout (Harvard Template style) that parses easily by Applicant Tracking Systems.
4.  **Instant PDF Export**: One-click download without watermarks.

## 4. Marketing Channels

### A. Community Engagement (The "Zero Budget" Approach)
*   **LinkedIn**: Share "Before/After" resume transformations. precise tips on resume writing.
*   **Reddit (r/resumes, r/jobs, r/webdev)**: transparently share the tool as a "side project using React" seeking feedback. Emphasize the "No Paywall/No Login" aspect.
*   **HackerNews**: "Show HN: I built a privacy-first resume builder because I hated paywalls."

### B. Product Launch Platforms
*   **Product Hunt**: A well-prepared launch can drive the initial 1,000 users.
*   **Dev.to / Hashnode**: Technical blog post "How I built a React Resume Builder with Real-time Preview".

### C. Content & SEO (Long Term)
*   Target keywords: "Free resume builder no login", "ATS friendly resume template free", "React resume builder".
*   Create a simple blog or "Tips" section on the site with resume writing guides.

## 5. Launch Roadmap

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Weeks 1-2)
*   Deploy to a public URL (Vercel/Netlify).
*   Share with close network and small Discord communities for bug hunting.
*   Optimize mobile responsiveness and PDF rendering quirks.

### Phase 2: Public Launch (Week 3)
*   **Day 1**: Product Hunt Launch (00:01 PST).
*   **Day 1**: HackerNews "Show HN" post.
*   **Day 2**: Twitter/LinkedIn threads sharing the story of building it.

### Phase 3: Growth & Iteration (Month 2+)
*   Analyze user feedback.
*   Add requested features (e.g., JSON import/export, more templates).
*   Explore "Buy Me a Coffee" monetization or optional premium templates.

## 6. Success Metrics (KPIs)
*   **Acquisition**: Daily Active Users (DAU), Unique Visitors.
*   **Engagement**: Time on Page, Completion Rate (clicks on "Download PDF").
*   **Virality**: Social Shares, Backlinks.
